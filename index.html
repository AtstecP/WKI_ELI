Impact of stress

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about how stress impacts people.

This is the man under stress

People usually think that stress has only a negative impact, but they are wrong. Always, when you hear the word "stress" you imagine struggle. Additionally, it could be associated with emotional health problems. However, there is an opposite side with benefits. People forget about it, so they try to avoid any kind of stress. Therefore, they lose the opportunity to improve themselves. Stress could have an ill impact and destroy you, but it could also give you a momentary boost and build resilience.

Painful effects of stress

Stress is able to destroy you physically and mentally. It is able to create diseases like high blood pressure, muscle weakness, and weight problems. American Psychological Association (2018) [1] said that under stress, the blood vessels dilate, thereby increasing the amount of blood and elevating blood pressure. In addition, sometimes people who have a lot of stress at work start going bald. My father is an example of this. Moreover, it has an effect on your mind. For instance, prolonged stress can develop depression, which contains sadness and hopelessness. In addition, people under stress lose their productivity, so they do not work as usual. However, stress has positive impacts as well.

Benefits of stress

In certain situations, stress can have some benefits. According to Peter Jaret’s article (2015) [2], experiments on rats showed improvements in learning and memory after a moderate amount of stress. Moreover, when people are in a stressful situation, especially when it is dangerous for their lives, their focus, performance, and alertness can level up for a short time. Therefore, a moderate amount of stress is necessary because that is how adaptation works. When people are stressed, their bodies adapt, so they create protection against this kind of stress. For instance, people who have jumped with a parachute are not afraid of heights; people who have had a lot of job interviews are not afraid of it; people who have had a lot of public presentations feel comfortable in public.

Conclusion

Overall, the impact of stress can be positive or negative, and it depends on the amount. If you get a moderate amount of stress, you can improve and adapt, but overdosing can bring physical and mental problems. People should control how much stress they get from their environment and use it to improve their skills, like public speaking.
